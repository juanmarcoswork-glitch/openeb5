Project overview

Brownsville Multifamily is a ground-up 200-unit Class B multifamily development in a USCIS-designated Rural Targeted Employment Area. Construction began in Q3 2024 with stabilization projected for Q1 2027. The project is structured under SEC Regulation D 506(c).

Open EB5 sponsors the offering; an established Texas-based developer holds construction obligation. Capital is contributed via a loan from the New Commercial Enterprise (NCE) to the Job-Creating Entity (JCE), per USCIS structure for Regional Center investments.

Why this project qualifies for EB-5

  • USCIS I-956F approved — formal project approval at the USCIS level reduces I-526E ambiguity.
  • Rural TEA set-aside — investors qualify for the $800K threshold AND a reserved visa category with faster processing.
  • Job creation calculated — economic study certifies 12+ direct/indirect jobs per investor, exceeding the 10-job requirement.
  • Capital preservation — senior position in capital stack; project covenants in NCE-JCE loan agreement.

Investor protections

  • Funds held in escrow until I-526E acceptance receipt.
  • Quarterly construction-progress reports with photographs and budget variance.
  • USCIS-required annual Form I-956G filings, copies provided to investors.
  • Independent fund administrator overseeing cash movement.

Risks (must read)

EB-5 investments are at-risk under U.S. immigration law. Capital is not guaranteed. Construction may experience delays, cost overruns, or market shifts. USCIS petition approval is not automatic. Read the Private Placement Memorandum in full and consult with independent immigration and securities counsel before subscribing.
